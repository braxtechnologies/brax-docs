# Governance

## 1. Overview
This document defines how the **[Project Name]** project is governed.  
The project follows one of two governance models:
- **Type A — Sponsor-Led:** Fully directed and maintained by the Sponsor Organization.  
- **Type B — Collaborative:** Jointly governed by the Sponsor and the community.

The governance model for this repository is:
> **Project Type:** [Type A – Sponsor-Led] or [Type B – Collaborative]

---

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

### Sponsor Organization
- Provides financial, infrastructure, and legal support.
- Maintains trademark, domain, and brand assets.
- In Sponsor-Led projects, defines strategic direction and release cadence.
- In Collaborative projects, holds up to two seats on the Governing Board.

### Governing Board (Type B only)
- Composed of Sponsor representatives and elected community members.
- Responsible for strategic direction, budget approval, and dispute resolution.
- Decisions are made by consensus; if not possible, a 2/3 majority vote applies.

### Technical Steering Committee (TSC)
- Leads technical decision-making and roadmap management.
- Composed of maintainers and lead engineers.
- Operates by consensus; may escalate unresolved issues to the Governing Board.

### Maintainers
- Trusted contributors with merge access.
- Vote on technical issues, feature adoption, and release planning.
- Expected to review code, mentor contributors, and uphold project standards.

### Contributors
- Anyone who submits a pull request, issue, or documentation update.
- Earn increased responsibilities through sustained, positive contributions.

---

## 3. Decision-Making Process
- **Day-to-day decisions:** Made by maintainers through consensus.  
- **Major technical decisions:** Reviewed by the TSC.  
- **Strategic or structural decisions:** Escalated to the Governing Board (Type B).  
- **Sponsor veto:** Limited to legal, security, and brand matters.  

---

## 4. Voting
- Each Maintainer and TSC member has one vote.  
- Votes may occur via public GitHub discussions or community calls.  
- A motion passes with a simple majority unless otherwise specified.

---

## 5. Transparency
All governance meetings, minutes, and decisions are documented publicly in:
- `/docs/governance/` or the project’s GitHub Discussions.
- Quarterly governance summaries are shared in the community newsletter.

---

## 6. Amendments
Changes to this governance structure require:
- Proposal through a pull request.
- 2/3 majority approval from the Governing Board (Type B) or Sponsor (Type A).
